The CIA Triad is a concept that includes three main principles. The letters in CIA stand for Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability. These concepts help IT professionals to protect data and maintain secure systems. The Confidentiality helps to ensure that only authorized users can access the system in place. This also includes limited users to only the data they need. This is typically done based on assigned roles, althought there are other methods. The second part, Integrity, refers to the accuracy of the data being stored or transmitted. The reliability of the data is important to maintain trust and smooth business operations. The last principle is Availability. This means that authorized users are able to access the data they are looking for. If data isn't able to be access, then business operations may halt as a result. This could lead to a loss in profit, downtime, and service disruptions.
